# Texas Instruments Evaluation Board Electronics

<img src="/images/electronics/ti_eval_board_1.jpg" alt="TI Launchpad with DRV8305 Booster Packs" width="500" />

*Launchpad F28069M with two DRV8305 Booster Packs - weight: 85g*

## Description

The TI evaluation boards provide a ready-to-use solution for dual brushless motor control, combining the Launchpad F28069M microcontroller with DRV8305 Booster Pack motor drivers.

### Key Specifications

- **Microcontroller**: TI Launchpad F28069M
- **Motor drivers**: 2x DRV8305 Booster Packs
- **Channels**: 2 independent brushless motors
- **Continuous current**: 15A per channel
- **Peak current**: 20A per channel
- **Supply voltage**: 5V - 45V (typically 24V)
- **Control frequency**: 10kHz Field Oriented Control (FOC) per channel
- **Main loop**: 1kHz control loop with realtime PC
- **Communication**: CAN or USB
- **Weight**: 85g

## Brushless Motor Control System

<img src="/images/electronics/bl_system_overview.jpg" alt="Brushless motor control system overview" width="600" />

The system consists of:
1. Power supply (24V)
2. TI Launchpad with motor driver boards
3. Brushless motors with encoders
4. Communication interface (CAN or USB)

## Field Oriented Control (FOC)

<img src="/images/electronics/foc.jpg" alt="Field Oriented Control diagram" width="600" />

The TI boards implement Field Oriented Control for efficient, high-performance motor control. FOC provides:

- Precise torque control
- Smooth operation at all speeds
- High efficiency
- Reduced acoustic noise

Learn more: [Teaching Old Motors New Tricks - TI Seminar](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VI7pdKrchM0)

## Launchpad Pin Assignment

<img src="/images/electronics/launchpad_pins_1.png" alt="Launchpad pin assignment" width="600" />

### GPIO Pins
- Status LEDs connected to digital GPIO pins
- GPIO 26 to ground disables motor drivers

### Analog Inputs
The Launchpad has 16 analog input channels:

**Per Booster Pack (7 channels each):**
- 3 phase voltages
- 3 phase currents
- 1 supply voltage

**Available channels:** ADCIN A6 and ADCIN B6

<Warning>
**Caution:** Do not connect 5V sources to analog input channels. Maximum voltage is 3.3V.
</Warning>

## Board Preparation

### Setting the Jumpers

<Steps>
  <Step title="Initial State">
    <img src="/images/electronics/ti_board_preparation_1.jpg" alt="Initial jumper configuration" width="500" />
    
    TI Launchpad with jumper settings as delivered.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Remove Jumpers">

    Remove the jumpers JP1, JP2, JP4, and JP5.

    </Warning>
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Install JP6">
    <img src="/images/electronics/ti_board_preparation_3.jpg" alt="Install JP6" width="500" />
    
    Install jumper JP6. Verify that JP7 and JP3 are also connected.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Encoder Connector Installation

<Steps>
  <Step title="Prepare Components">
    <img src="/images/electronics/launchpad_preparation_1.jpg" alt="Prepare encoder wires" width="400" />
    
    Gather encoder wires (0.14 mm² Kabeltronik) and 5-pole Hirose DF13 connectors.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Solder Connector">
    <img src="/images/electronics/launchpad_preparation_2.jpg" alt="Solder wires to connector" width="400" />
    
    Solder wires to the Hirose connector following the pin assignment.
    
    <img src="/images/electronics/pin_assignment_connector.png" alt="Pin assignment" width="400" />
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Apply Heat Shrink">
    <img src="/images/electronics/launchpad_preparation_3.jpg" alt="Heat shrink on wires" width="400" />
    
    Apply thin heat shrink to red and yellow wires.
    
    <img src="/images/electronics/launchpad_preparation_4.jpg" alt="Larger heat shrink" width="400" />
    
    Apply larger heat shrink around all wires.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Prepare Second Cable">
    <img src="/images/electronics/launchpad_preparation_5.jpg" alt="Second cable" width="400" />
    
    Repeat for the second encoder cable.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Locate Encoder Pins">
    <img src="/images/electronics/launchpad_preparation_6.jpg" alt="Encoder pins" width="400" />
    
    Find the encoder pins on the Launchpad. The two ports are labeled QEP_A and QEP_B.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Bend Pins">
    <img src="/images/electronics/launchpad_preparation_7.jpg" alt="Bend pins" width="400" />
    
    Use flat pliers to bend the pins down by 90 degrees.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Solder and Finish">
    <img src="/images/electronics/launchpad_preparation_8.jpg" alt="Solder encoder wires" width="400" />
    
    Solder the encoder wires and apply heat shrink.
    
    <img src="/images/electronics/pin_assignment_launchpad.png" alt="Launchpad pin assignment" width="400" />
    
    <img src="/images/electronics/launchpad_preparation_9.jpg" alt="Completed Launchpad" width="500" />
    
    The Launchpad is now prepared.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Booster Pack Preparation

### Modifying Screw Terminals

<Note>
This modification is optional but recommended for cleaner wire routing. Both booster packs must be connected to the power supply.
</Note>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Initial State">
    <img src="/images/electronics/booster_preparation_3.jpg" alt="Screw terminals" width="400" />
    
    Booster pack screw terminals to be modified.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Loosen Screws">
    <img src="/images/electronics/booster_preparation_4.jpg" alt="Loosen screws" width="400" />
    
    Loosen screws on the screw terminal as much as possible.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Bend Metal Clips">
    <img src="/images/electronics/booster_preparation_5.jpg" alt="Bend clips" width="400" />
    
    Use a small screwdriver to bend the metal clips outwards.
    
    <img src="/images/electronics/booster_preparation_6.jpg" alt="All clips bent" width="400" />
    
    Repeat for all 5 metal clips.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Remove Screws">
    <img src="/images/electronics/booster_preparation_7.jpg" alt="Remove screws" width="400" />
    
    Push on the screws from the bottom and remove them. Keep screws for reinstallation.
    
    <img src="/images/electronics/booster_preparation_8.jpg" alt="Remove metal clips" width="400" />
    
    Remove the metal clips.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Modify Covers">
    <img src="/images/electronics/booster_preparation_9.jpg" alt="Mark holes" width="400" />
    
    Mark hole locations on the two-pin power terminal with a pen.
    
    <img src="/images/electronics/booster_preparation_10.jpg" alt="Remove covers" width="400" />
    
    Push plastic covers inwards to remove them.
    
    <img src="/images/electronics/booster_preparation_11.jpg" alt="Removed covers" width="400" />
    
    <img src="/images/electronics/booster_preparation_12.jpg" alt="Drill holes" width="400" />
    
    Drill the plastic cover at marked locations. Start with 2mm, then 2.5mm, then 3mm drill bit.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Reinstall Covers">
    <img src="/images/electronics/booster_preparation_13.jpg" alt="Reinstall covers" width="400" />
    
    Reinstall plastic covers. The 2-pin power terminal cover installs normally. Rotate the 3-pin cover 180 degrees.
    
    <img src="/images/electronics/booster_preparation_14.jpg" alt="Bottom booster pack" width="500" />
    
    On the bottom booster pack, only modify the 3-pin phase terminal. Leave the 2-pin power terminal unchanged.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Power Supply Wire Preparation

<Steps>
  <Step title="Strip Insulation">
    <img src="/images/electronics/booster_preparation_15.jpg" alt="Cut insulation" width="400" />
    
    Use 1mm² wire. Carefully cut into the insulation with a cutter and rotate.
    
    <img src="/images/electronics/booster_preparation_16.jpg" alt="Cut with offset" width="400" />
    
    Cut the insulation again with a 5mm offset.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Remove Insulation">
    <img src="/images/electronics/booster_preparation_17.jpg" alt="Remove insulation" width="400" />
    
    Carefully cut and remove the separated insulation.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Twist and Solder">
    <img src="/images/electronics/booster_preparation_18.jpg" alt="Twist and solder" width="400" />
    
    Twist the wire and apply solder.
    
    <img src="/images/electronics/booster_preparation_19.jpg" alt="Ground wire" width="400" />
    
    Repeat for the power supply ground wire.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Shorten and Connect">
    <img src="/images/electronics/booster_preparation_20.jpg" alt="Shorten wires" width="400" />
    
    Shorten wires to about 7cm, remove 5mm insulation, and apply solder.
    
    <img src="/images/electronics/booster_preparation_21.jpg" alt="Determine wire length" width="400" />
    
    Place booster packs on Launchpad to determine wire length.
    
    <img src="/images/electronics/booster_preparation_22.jpg" alt="Install connectors" width="500" />
    
    Install 4mm connectors for power supply.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Motor Phase Wire Preparation

<Steps>
  <Step title="Prepare Wires">
    <img src="/images/electronics/booster_preparation_23.jpg" alt="Motor phase wires" width="400" />
    
    Prepare six motor phase wires using 0.5mm² wire and 2mm connectors.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Solder and Label">
    <img src="/images/electronics/booster_preparation_24.jpg" alt="Solder and label" width="400" />
    
    Solder connectors, apply heat shrink, and label motor phases.

  <Step title="Install Wires">
    <img src="/images/electronics/booster_preparation_25.jpg" alt="Install wires" width="500" />
    
    Install power wire and motor phase wires on booster packs.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## CAN Communication

<img src="/images/electronics/can_card.jpg" alt="Dual channel CAN card" width="400" />

<img src="/images/electronics/can_connector.jpg" alt="CAN connector" width="400" />

### Setup

1. Install a dual channel CAN card in your computer (e.g., PEAK PCI Express)
2. Connect using a 9-pin D-sub connector
3. Flash the Launchpad via USB with DIP switch: ON - ON - ON
4. Set DIP switches to ON - ON - OFF (run from flash)
5. Control motors via CAN at 1kHz

See [Wiring](/electronics/wiring) for detailed CAN connector wiring information.

## Comparison with Micro Driver

<img src="/images/electronics/launchpad_comparison_2.jpg" alt="Size comparison" width="500" />

The Micro Driver Electronics provides the same capabilities as the TI boards with these differences:

| Feature | TI Boards | Micro Driver |
|---------|-----------|-------------|
| **Size** | Large | 10x smaller |
| **Weight** | 85g | 13g (6x lighter) |
| **Programming** | Onboard USB | External JTAG |
| **Communication** | CAN/USB | SPI (Master Board) |
| **Cost** | Lower (evaluation pricing) | Higher (production) |

See [Micro Driver](/electronics/micro-driver) for more information.
