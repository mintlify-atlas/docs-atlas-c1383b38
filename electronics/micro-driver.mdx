# Micro Driver Electronics

<img src="/images/electronics/micro_driver_v2_1.jpg" alt="Micro Driver v2.1" width="500" />

## Overview

The Micro Driver is a miniaturized version of the Texas Instruments evaluation boards, providing the same motor control capabilities in a compact form factor ideal for integrated robot designs.

### Key Specifications

- **Type**: Dual brushless motor driver
- **Control frequency**: 10kHz torque control per motor
- **Communication**: SPI with Master Board at 1kHz
- **Board layers**: 6-layer FR4
- **Copper thickness**: 35μm
- **Dimensions**: 51mm × 50mm
- **Weight**: 13g
- **Input voltage**: 5V - 32V (typically 24V)
- **Current rating**: Similar to TI boards (15A continuous, 20A peak)

## LED Status Indicators

### GREEN/BLUE LED (System and Motor Status)

- **Off**: System disabled
- **On**: System enabled
- **Slow blinking**: Motor enabled
- **Fast blinking**: Aligning motor

### RED LED

Turns on when there is an error condition.

## Size Comparison

<img src="/images/electronics/motor_driver_comparison.png" alt="Motor driver comparison" width="500" />

The Micro Driver is significantly smaller than the TI evaluation boards:

| Feature | TI Boards | Micro Driver | Improvement |
|---------|-----------|--------------|-------------|
| **Size** | Large format | 51mm × 50mm | 10x smaller |
| **Weight** | 85g | 13g | 6.5x lighter |
| **Programming** | Onboard USB | External JTAG | More compact |
| **Communication** | CAN/USB | SPI | Faster, integrated |

## Assembly Instructions

The Micro Drivers arrive from the manufacturer without connectors and precision resistors. These components must be manually soldered.

### Precision Resistor Soldering

The six precision resistors (7mΩ) must be soldered on the back of the Micro Driver for phase current measurement.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Prepare Board">
    <img src="/images/electronics/micro_driver_resistor_soldering_preparation.jpg" alt="Resistor preparation" width="500" />
    
    Position the board with the back side facing up. Identify the six resistor locations.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Initial Tack">
    <img src="/images/electronics/micro_driver_resistor_soldering.jpg" alt="Resistor soldering" width="500" />
    
    **Trick:** Apply solder at the tip of one pad to initially fix the resistor. Then solder the other side and return to properly fix the initial side.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Complete Soldering">
    <img src="/images/electronics/micro_driver_resistor_final.jpg" alt="Completed resistors" width="500" />
    
    Ensure all six resistors are properly soldered with good solder joints.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Hirose Connector Soldering

<img src="/images/electronics/micro_driver_stack_1.jpg" alt="Micro Driver stack with right angle connectors" width="500" />

Two connector options are available:

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Right Angle Connectors">
    **Recommended for stacked configurations**
    
    <img src="/images/electronics/micro_driver_hirose_connectors_1.jpg" alt="Right angle connectors" width="500" />
    
    - Mounted on the **bottom side** of the board
    - Allows connecting/disconnecting without disassembling stack
    - Better for compact robot designs
    - Part: DF13-5P-1.25DS(20)
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Straight Connectors">
    **Alternative option**
    
    <img src="/images/electronics/micro_driver_hirose_connectors_1.jpg" alt="Straight connectors" width="500" />
    
    - Mounted on the **top side** of the board
    - Simpler soldering process
    - Compatible with same wiring interface
    - Part: DF13-5S-1.25C
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

#### Encoder Connectors (2x per board)

<Steps>
  <Step title="Position Board">
    <img src="/images/electronics/micro_driver_hirose_preparation.jpg" alt="Position board in vise" width="500" />
    
    Use a vise to clamp the board and locate the Hirose connector positions.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Solder Connectors">
    <img src="/images/electronics/micro_driver_hirose_soldering.jpg" alt="Solder Hirose connectors" width="500" />
    
    Solder the two encoder connectors (5-pin each) onto the board. Ensure proper alignment before soldering.
  </Step>
</Steps>

#### SPI Connector (1x per board)

<img src="/images/electronics/micro_driver_hirose_connectors_2.jpg" alt="SPI connector" width="500" />

Solder the SPI communication connector (5-pin) using the same process as the encoder connectors.

<Note>
**Pin Assignment:** Both right angle and straight connectors maintain identical pin assignments when mounted as specified, ensuring compatibility with the actuator module wiring interface.
</Note>

### Motor Phase and Power Wire Preparation

#### Power Wires

<Steps>
  <Step title="Solder Pin Header">
    <img src="/images/electronics/power_preparation_1.jpg" alt="Power pin header" width="400" />
    
    Solder a two-pin right angle connector to the power terminals.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Add Extension Wires">
    <img src="/images/electronics/power_preparation_2.jpg" alt="Power extension wires" width="400" />
    
    Shorten the pins, solder extension wires (0.5mm²), and apply heat shrink.
  </Step>
</Steps>

#### Motor Phase Wires

<Steps>
  <Step title="Prepare Pin and Mating Connector">
    <img src="/images/electronics/motor_phase_preparation_1.jpg" alt="Right angle pin and mating connector" width="400" />
    
    <img src="/images/electronics/motor_phase_preparation_2.jpg" alt="Use mating connector" width="400" />
    
    Use a mating connector to hold the right angle pin during soldering.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Solder Pins">
    <img src="/images/electronics/motor_phase_preparation_3.jpg" alt="Orient and solder" width="400" />
    
    Orient the pin as desired, apply solder, and shorten the pins.
    
    <img src="/images/electronics/motor_phase_preparation_4.jpg" alt="All six connections" width="400" />
    
    Repeat for all six motor phase connections.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Add Extension Wires">
    <img src="/images/electronics/motor_phase_preparation_5.jpg" alt="Extension wires" width="400" />
    
    Apply extension wires (0.5mm²) and heat shrink.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Attach Connectors">
    <img src="/images/electronics/motor_phase_preparation_6.jpg" alt="Motor phase connectors" width="400" />
    
    Attach 2mm motor phase connectors and label the wires. On one board, attach a 2-pin Hirose wire to power the Master Board.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Programming and Flashing

<img src="/images/electronics/udrive_programmable.jpg" alt="Micro Driver with JTAG connection" width="600" />

The Micro Driver boards are programmed using a JTAG emulator connected via USB.

### Flash Cable Wiring

<Warning>
**Important:** A long cable may cause communication problems when flashing. A cable length of 10 cm works well.
</Warning>

<img src="/images/electronics/wire_programmable.jpg" alt="Flash cable" width="600" />

| Emulator Connector | Micro Driver Connector |
|-------------------|------------------------|
| <img src="/images/electronics/udrive_wireng_programmable.png" width="400" /> | <img src="/images/electronics/udrive_wireng_micro_driver.png" width="300" /> |

### JTAG Connection

<img src="/images/electronics/micro_driver_v2_1_jtag.jpg" alt="JTAG connector location" width="500" />

The JTAG connector is an 8-pin header with 1.27mm pitch on the Micro Driver board.

### Programming Steps

1. Connect the JTAG emulator to your computer via USB
2. Connect the flash cable between the emulator and Micro Driver
3. Ensure the Micro Driver is powered (5V-32V)
4. Use Code Composer Studio or appropriate IDE to flash firmware
5. Verify successful programming via LED status

## Communication with Master Board

The Micro Driver communicates with the Master Board via SPI at 1kHz.

## Micro Driver Stack Configuration

<img src="/images/electronics/micro_driver_stack_front.jpg" alt="Micro Driver stack" width="500" />

Multiple Micro Driver boards can be stacked for compact multi-motor configurations:

- Up to 6 boards per Master Board
- Right angle connectors recommended for stacks
- SPI daisy-chain connection between boards
- Shared power distribution

## Advantages Over TI Boards

1. **Size and Weight**: 10x smaller, 6x lighter
2. **Integration**: Designed to fit inside robot structures
3. **SPI Communication**: Faster, synchronized control with Master Board
4. **Stackability**: Multiple boards in compact configuration
5. **Production Ready**: Professional PCB for final robot designs
